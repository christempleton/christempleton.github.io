Join our team!

Are you interested in studying animal communication, cognition,  behavioral ecology, or conservation? Join us! We are always excited to have motivated and engaged students of all levels join our research group.


Our lab is composed of a diverse group of researchers connected by a passion for understanding the natural world. We strongly believe that diverse backgrounds bring diverse perspectives that in turn inspire intellectual creativity and facilite scientific progress for all. We welcome and support all people, all races, all ethnicities, and all gender identities in the lab. We recognize that systemic racism and discrimination permeate both society and the academy, creating major challenges for the BIPOC and LGBTQIA+ communities, and we especially encourage these students to join us. Our lab group is a safe and supportive space that welcomes and celebrates all axes of diversity. .


Undergrads:

Undergraduates interested in gaining research experience during the summer or school year should contact me directly via email. We have lots of different opportunities for undergrads of all levels to learn new techniques in acoustic analysis, help with field and lab work, or develop their own capstone thesis research projects. Paid undergraduate positions through workstudy or summer research stipends are often available in the lab.

Graduate students:

I am recruiting graduate students to join the lab at Western Washington. If you are interested in pursuing MS research in the lab, please  contact me directly via email. I also remain happy to co-supervise PhD students or serve on thesis committees at other institutions.


Postdocs:

We will soon have an opening for an NSF-funded postdoctoral position to examine the effects of traffic noise on avian cognition. Please get in touch if you are interested!

I am also always happy to work with interested researchers to write grant or fellowship applications to support other postdoctoral research projects in the lab.
